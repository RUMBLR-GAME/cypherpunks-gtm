01 / Briefv2.0 · 30d window

Read this first.

If a teammate joins on day 14, this is the page they read.

What we're launching. 10,000 pixel art keys on Ethereum, ERC-721, auctioned one per day for ~22 years. Holding a CypherPunk grants curation votes, gated commentary, and listing rights across CURAT'D, HASH'D, LIMIT'D. Selling auto-removes you from the curated roster, on-contract. Skin in the game is the entry condition, not a slogan.

Three goals, equally weighted. A real day-1 auction with real bidders. Credibility with the collectors who buy 1/1s on SuperRare. A founding artist roster of 12-20 names whose presence on CURAT'D legitimizes the platform.

Hard constraints. 30-day clock. Lean team. No paid KOL deals. No allowlist hacks. No Discord.

Three sentences. Whole pitch.

For collectors

"The contract removes you when you sell. That's the whole pitch."

For artists

"Curation as a primitive, not a permission."

For anyone, in any room

"One auction a day, twenty two years, holders decide the roster."

The one sentence to internalize.

Goal of these 30 days is not to make people excited about CypherPunks — it is to make 100 specific people feel they would be missing something by not paying attention. Excitement is a bell curve. Missing-out is a hook.

12 / Risks & failure modespre-mortem

What can go wrong.

Read once at start. Reread at D14.

Founding roster slips below 8

Reduce reveal scope. Frame as "founding artists, week 1 cohort, more on the way." Don't pretend the number is bigger than it is.

MED

Cypherpunk-name tension goes public

The Punk9059 / 6529 pre-emptive DM is your insurance. If it still escalates: respond once, in public, with the manifesto's name-distinction passage. Do not engage repeatedly.

HIGH

No press placement lands

Lean harder on founding-artist amplification at D15. The artists ARE the story; press is bonus. Don't beg.

MED

Auction one clears under expectation

Don't apologize. Don't comment on price. Welcome the holder, talk about what they unlocked. Auction two starts in the same minute — direct attention forward.

MED

Auction one fails to attract any bid

Reserve price logic should prevent this. If it happens: be honest, post the bid history, ship auction two on schedule. The 22-year clock buys you forgiveness for one bad day.

LOW

Smart contract bug surfaces

Pause. Communicate immediately. Audit fix in public. Postpone, don't paper over. Trust is the actual product.

LOW

Founding artist publicly distances after reveal

Honor it. Acknowledge once, briefly, without spin. Remove from roster on-contract. A graceful departure is content if handled right.

MED

Twitter Space goes badly

Acceptable if any one moment is quotable. Plan three quotable lines in advance. The Space's job is to produce a clip, not a vibe.

MED

ETH market drawdown during launch week

The 22-year cadence is your shield. "Auction one happens regardless of market conditions, and so do auctions 2 through 8,000."

MED

Founder fatigue / burnout in week 4

Predictable. Pre-write D26-30 tweets in week 2. Eat. Sleep. The auction runs whether or not you're optimizing.

HIGH

One of the bespoke DMs leaks publicly

Each is written to be readable in public. If leaked, no scandal — they're respectful and substantive by design.

LOW

Two beliefs to hold

One. The 30 days are not the project. The project is 22 years. If day one is a 6 out of 10, that is fine. Don't trade the 22 years for the 30 days.

Two. Every shortcut you'll be tempted to take — paid amps, allowlist hacks, Discord drama, KOL deals — is a tax you pay against the manifesto. The manifesto is the most valuable asset of this entire launch. Defend it harder than you defend the price.

Final note for the version of you reading this on D26 at 23:00 UTC. Whatever happened, you did the work. The contract will run. Auction two is already queued. Go to bed. Tomorrow is just one of 8,030 days.
